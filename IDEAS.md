# HyperGal — riff ideas

Extensions of the existing body of work. The riff grammar across the current
eleven pieces:

- **Remove the player** → the system plays itself to equilibrium (*hydra*,
  *Vectrex Asteroids*)
- **Remove the protagonist, keep the antagonists** → *Only Invaders*,
  *Only Centipede*
- **Invert the verb** → break becomes build (*unbreakable*), destroy becomes
  accrete (*Asteroids* welding)
- **Make it symmetric / mutual** → *Mutual Command*, the four-edge
  *Only Invaders*
- **No win, no lose, floored-and-capped** → it runs forever
- **Death leaves a painting** → stains, bone memory, sediment
  (*hunt prey paint*, *hydra*)

## Leading ideas

### Only Ghosts — *Pac-Man · remove the protagonist*
The next entry in the *Only* series. Four ghosts run their real
Blinky/Pinky/Inky/Clyde targeting personalities — but there's no Pac-Man to
chase. They orbit the absent center, scatter to their corners, cross paths,
trace where the player *would* have been. The dots never get eaten; the maze
slowly fills with the four colored pursuit-trails as the artifact. Their whole
character was always defined by a thing they're chasing — so remove it.

### deuce — *Pong · remove the player + invert the stakes*
The urtext. Two paddles that have learned to never miss. The score is frozen
forever; the *rally* is the piece. Each volley the ball heats up and
accelerates until it's a phosphor smear strobing between two walls, then a
fault resets it cool. The whole drama is a point that can't end.

### a lander that never lands — *Lunar Lander · remove the player*
Pairs directly with *Vectrex Asteroids* — same black-and-white vector monitor.
An autonomous craft computing fuel-optimal burns over procedurally
regenerating terrain, descending toward a pad, kissing it, lifting off,
drifting to the next. Residue: scorched pads, spent-fuel haze, and the faint
arcs of every trajectory it's flown layering up like a long-exposure.

### wellkeeper — *Tetris · invert + equilibrium*
The *unbreakable* move applied to the falling-block stack. An AI packs pieces
perfectly, but it's tuned to *hold the stack at a fixed line* rather than win —
clears keep the surface level, exactly like the build-front clamp in
*unbreakable*. Cleared rows don't vanish to empty; they compost into a colored
sediment band at the bottom that grows into geology. The game defined by
topping out, made unable to top out.

### stake — *Qix · symmetric + death-leaves-a-painting*
Two adversaries each claiming area, slicing the plane into hard-edged colored
regions. No score, just the slow partition. The mechanic *is* the painting —
the most directly generative-art of the set, closest to *obstacles* and
*all-wall* but with a real adversarial engine underneath.

## Shorter sketches

- **Only Formation** — *Galaga*: divers that endlessly form, peel off, loop,
  and re-rank with no player to shoot at. A murmuration that remembers it used
  to be an attack wave.
- **light** — *Tron cycles*: autonomous cycles partitioning the arena into a
  Voronoi territory map; a crash floods the loser's enclosed cells with color.
  (Snake-adjacent, so possibly redundant with *hydra*.)
- **Frogger as flow** — autonomous frogs reading endless traffic/river
  flow-fields; roadkill stains accumulate into a heat-map of the dangerous
  lanes.

## Recommendation

Build **Only Ghosts** next — cleanest fit with the existing series, and the
"AI chasing an absent center" concept is doing real conceptual work.
